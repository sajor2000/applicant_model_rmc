2. Introduction and Clinical Need

The Challenge

Medical school admissions committees face an overwhelming volume of applications each year. At Rush Medical College:

  • Thousands of applications require thorough review
  • Each application contains multiple essays, academic records, and experiences
  • Human reviewers may experience fatigue and inconsistency
  • The process is time-intensive and resource-demanding

The Solution

An AI-assisted system that:

  • Analyzes applications using structured demographic data and AI-powered essay analysis
  • Predicts human reviewer scores with high accuracy
  • Categorizes applicants into four actionable quartiles
  • Provides consistent, fatigue-free initial screening

Plain English Summary

What We Actually Did:

  1. We took basic information about each applicant (age, gender, state, etc.)
  2. We looked at their volunteer and research hours
  3. We had AI read their essays and score them on qualities like motivation and leadership
  4. We transformed all numbers to be on similar scales (so 1000 hours doesn't overwhelm 10 hours)
  5. We created summary statistics (like the average of all essay scores)
  6. We fed these 32 pieces of information into a machine learning model
  7. The model learned patterns from 838 past applicants to predict reviewer decisions

What We Did NOT Use: GPA, MCAT scores, specific school names, or any identifying information.

3.4 Model Development

Three-Stage Cascade Architecture

The model uses a cascade approach with three sequential binary classifiers:

  1. Stage 1: Bottom Quartile (Q4) vs. Upper Quartiles (Q1-Q3)
  2. Stage 2: Among upper quartiles: Third Quartile (Q3) vs. Top Half (Q1-Q2)
  3. Stage 3: Among top half: Second Quartile (Q2) vs. Top Quartile (Q1)

Algorithm Used: XGBoost - a powerful machine learning algorithm that builds decision trees and naturally handles missing data by learning the best path for missing values.

6. Limitations and Future Work

TRIPOD-AI Item 18: Limitations

  • Training Data: Model trained on 2022-2023 data may need updating as applicant pools evolve
  • Essay Analysis: Relies on GPT-4o which may miss nuanced human elements
