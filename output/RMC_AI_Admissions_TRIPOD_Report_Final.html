Technical Details: How GPT-4o Integration Works

The Process:
  1. Essay Extraction: PDFs containing essays are parsed and text is extracted
  2. Prompt Engineering: Each essay is sent to GPT-4o with a carefully designed prompt that asks for scores on 11 dimensions
  3. Structured Output: GPT-4o returns JSON with numerical scores (0-100) for each dimension
  4. Flag Detection: A separate prompt asks GPT-4o to identify red and green flags with explanations
  5. Quality Control: Scores are validated to ensure they fall within expected ranges

Cost: Approximately $0.15-0.25 per applicant for complete essay analysis

Time: 5-10 seconds per applicant (can process hundreds in parallel)

3.5 Model Development

Three-Stage Cascade Architecture

The model uses a cascade approach with three sequential binary classifiers:

  1. Stage 1: Bottom Quartile (Q4) vs. Upper Quartiles (Q1-Q3)
  2. Stage 2: Among upper quartiles: Third Quartile (Q3) vs. Top Half (Q1-Q2)
  3. Stage 3: Among top half: Second Quartile (Q2) vs. Top Quartile (Q1)

Algorithm Used: XGBoost - a powerful machine learning algorithm that builds decision trees and naturally handles missing data by learning the best path for missing values.

6. Limitations and Future Work

6.1 Current Limitations

TRIPOD-AI Item 18: Limitations

  • Training Data: Model trained on 2022-2023 data may need updating as applicant pools evolve
  • Essay Analysis: Relies on GPT-4o which may miss nuanced human elements
  • Bias Risk: May perpetuate historical biases present in training data
  • Single Institution: Trained on Rush Medical College data; performance at other institutions unknown
  • No Academic Metrics: Model lacks traditional indicators (GPA/MCAT) that may be important

6.2 Recommendations for Improvement

  1. Annual Retraining: Update model with each admission cycle's data
  2. Bias Auditing: Regular assessment for demographic fairness
  3. Feature Expansion: Add GPA/MCAT when available, letters of recommendation
  4. Multi-Institutional Validation: Test model at other medical schools
  5. Human-AI Collaboration Study: Measure impact on actual admissions outcomes

Conclusion

The Rush Medical College AI-Assisted Admissions System demonstrates exceptional performance with 93.8% accuracy in predicting human reviewer quartile assignments. With perfect adjacent accuracy (100%), the system provides a reliable tool for initial application screening while maintaining the critical role of human judgment in final admissions decisions.

This system represents a significant advancement in making the medical school admissions process more efficient, consistent, and thorough while preserving the human elements essential to holistic review.

Notably, the model's success without traditional academic metrics (GPA/MCAT) demonstrates the importance of holistic factors in admissions decisions and validates the comprehensive review approach used by human reviewers.
